What is LeanGains?

A brief rundown


Lean Gains is a diet that was made popular by Martin Berkhan. It is a diet and workout methodology in which a person cycles high calorie and low calorie days while also cycling macro-nutrient(Carbs, Fats & Protein) intake. The diet also utilizes Intermittent Fasting or "IF" for short, which consists of a period of fasting and a period of feasting, typically done by first fasting for sixteen hours and then eating your days worth of calories in an eight our window. The goal of Lean Gains is to create an environment that is favorable for body composition during all phases of dieting, be it gaining weight, losing weight or body recomposition. Different variations of this diet exist, but they all include some form of intermittent fasting and usually calorie and macro-nutrient cycling as well. You can read more about the diet at leangains.com and use the calculator below to determine your daily calorie and macro-nutrient goals.

    Activity Level

    The physical activity level (PAL) is a way to express a person's daily physical activity as a number, and is used to estimate a person's total energy expenditure. In combination with the basal metabolic rate, it can be used to compute the amount of food energy a person needs to consume in order to maintain a particular lifestyle.

    Sedentary: A person who is rarely active, such as someone working an office job for the majority of their day.
    Lightly Active: A person performing light exercise or sports 1-3 times per week.
    Moderately Active: A person performing moderate exercise or sports 3-5 times per week.
    Very Active: A person performing intense exercise or sports 6-7 times per week.
    Extremely Active: A person performing very intense exercise or sports every day who also has a physically demanding job.
